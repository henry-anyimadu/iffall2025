Jollof Rice

Origin: West Africa | Time: 1 hour 30 minutes

About

Jollof rice is a popular dish in West Africa, especially in Nigeria, Ghana, and Senegal. It is a one-pot dish of rice cooked in a tomato-based sauce with various spices. The origin of jollof rice is a topic of friendly debate among West African nations, with each country having its own unique version of the recipe. This recipe is a Nigerian-style jollof rice, known for its smoky flavor and vibrant red color.

Ingredients

  • 4 cups long-grain parboiled rice
  • 6 large red bell peppers (tatashe)
  • 3 medium-sized tomatoes
  • 2 scotch bonnet peppers (ata rodo)
  • 2 large onions
  • 1/2 cup vegetable oil
  • 3 cloves of garlic
  • 1-inch ginger root
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 3 cups chicken or beef stock
  • 1 teaspoon curry powder
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 2 bay leaves
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 stock cube (Maggi or Knorr)
  • Water, as needed

Instructions

Step 1

Blend the red bell peppers, tomatoes, and scotch bonnet peppers until smooth. In a large pot, heat the vegetable oil and add the sliced onions. Fry until translucent, then add the blended pepper mix and tomato paste. Cook for about 20-25 minutes until the sauce is reduced and the oil starts to separate.

Step 2

While the sauce is cooking, wash the rice thoroughly until the water runs clear. Add the chicken or beef stock to the reduced sauce, followed by the curry powder, dried thyme, bay leaves, salt, and stock cube. Stir well and bring to a boil.

Step 3

Add the washed rice to the boiling sauce. Stir to combine, ensuring the rice is fully submerged. Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid and reduce the heat to low. Let it simmer for 30-40 minutes, or until the rice is cooked through. For a smoky flavor, you can let the rice burn slightly at the bottom (the "party rice" effect).
